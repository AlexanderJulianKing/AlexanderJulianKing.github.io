Project Soothsayer

LLM Evaluation & Arena ELO Prediction

A multi-benchmark evaluation suite that predicts how well LLMs will perform in the Chatbot Arena—before anyone votes. Four custom benchmarks join 20+ public leaderboards, feed through a per-cell imputation engine, and emerge as ELO predictions within 1.3% of actual scores.

Why does this exist?

The Problem

Arena.ai captures something no benchmark can—real human preference, derived from millions of blind head-to-head votes. But what it measures is difficult to pin down. It isn’t model intelligence in any strict sense, and it isn’t objective quality. It reflects what people prefer, which is a fundamentally different signal. Traditional benchmarks don’t measure it, and it’s not clear they can.

Why It’s Hard

Benchmarks evaluate well-defined capabilities: coding, mathematics, logical reasoning. Human preference is nearly a black box. Intelligence is multidimensional and context-dependent—the gap between “this model performs well” and “people prefer this model” is real and nontrivial. Compounding this, the benchmark matrix is roughly 40% sparse—not every model is evaluated on every test.

5. From Features to Predictions

Feature selection and regression turn imputed benchmark scores into Arena ELO estimates.

1.

Tree-Based Feature Selection

LightGBM ranks 65+ features by importance gain. The 1-SE rule picks the most parsimonious subset—typically 10–15 features.

2.

50/50 Blend: ARD + Huber

With only 123 training samples, nonlinear models overfit. The final model is a 50/50 blend of ARD Regression (which zeros out irrelevant features) and Huber Regression (which is robust to outliers).
